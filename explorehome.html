Top Places to Visit in Chennai

Marina Beach

One of the longest urban beaches in the world, perfect for evening strolls and sunrise views.

Kapaleeshwarar Temple

A historic Dravidian-style temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, known for its intricate carvings and religious significance.

Fort St. George

Built by the British in 1644, this fort now houses a museum showcasing colonial history and artifacts.

Valluvar kottam

"A grand monument in Chennai, stands as a tribute to the legendary Tamil poet Thiruvalluvar, featuring a majestic chariot-shaped auditorium and intricate stone inscriptions of all 1,330 Thirukkural verses—a true marvel of Tamil heritage and architectural brilliance."

Government Chennai museum

"Step into history at the Government Museum, Chennai – where art, culture, and heritage come alive! "

Santhome Cathedral Basilica,

" A breathtaking blend of history and spirituality, stands as a magnificent tribute to St. Thomas, one of Jesus' twelve apostles, offering visitors a serene escape with its stunning neo-Gothic architecture and coastal charm."

Arulmigu Sri Parthasarathyswamy Temple

A centuries-old marvel in Chennai, stands as a testament to divine devotion and intricate Dravidian architecture. Adorned with vibrant sculptures and rich history, this sacred temple echoes the tales of Lord Krishna in his majestic charioteer form
